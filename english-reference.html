Three Methods of Evidence Integration

1. DIRECT QUOTE (use sparingly - most powerful words only)
The author emphasizes determination when she states, "I will not give up, no matter the cost" (Jones 89). This powerful declaration reveals her unwavering commitment.
2. PARAPHRASE (use frequently - restate in your words)
The protagonist demonstrates courage by refusing to abandon her mission despite overwhelming obstacles (Jones 89). This persistence highlights her strength.
3. SUMMARY (for broader points - condense larger sections)
Throughout the novel, the main character faces numerous challenges that test her resolve (Jones 85-92). These experiences shape her into a stronger person.

The Quote Sandwich

  • Top bun ‚Äì Introduce: Give context + speaker.

  • Meat ‚Äì Quote with citation: Use only the key words and cite correctly.
    He notices that the apple "changed, just for an instant" (Lowry 24).
  • Bottom bun ‚Äì Explain: Connect the quote back to your claim.
    This shift shows his growing awareness that his world is not as perfect as it seems.

Basic Essay Structure

INTRODUCTION ‚Üí BODY PARAGRAPHS (x2‚Äì3+) ‚Üí CONCLUSION

Thesis Statement Formula

Literary analysis thesis:
In Title by Author, the author uses device 1 and device 2 to show that big idea/theme.

Example:
In Of Mice and Men, John Steinbeck uses symbolism and foreshadowing to show that the American Dream is often unreachable for ordinary people.

TEAL / TDEC Paragraph Structure

  • T ‚Äì Topic Sentence: Your mini-claim for the paragraph.
  • E ‚Äì Evidence: Quote, paraphrase, or summary from the text.
  • A ‚Äì Analysis: Explain how/why it proves your point.
  • L ‚Äì Link: Connect back to thesis or transition to next idea.

Sample Analytical Paragraph

Topic: How a character changes

At the beginning of the novel, Jonas follows the rules without question, but his exposure to memory forces him to think independently. When he first receives the memory of war, he realizes that his community has been hiding pain from him and admits, "I didn't know there could be so much suffering" (Lowry 121). This moment reveals how protected and naive Jonas has been his entire life. By confronting the truth, he begins to question whether "sameness" is worth the cost of losing real emotions. Ultimately, this transformation shows that true maturity requires facing uncomfortable realities rather than living in ignorance.

‚Ä¢ Missing subject or verb

Run-on Sentences & Fixes

Run-on: She loved reading she hated writing essays.

Fix with:
  • Period: She loved reading. She hated writing essays.
  • Comma + FANBOYS: She loved reading, but she hated writing essays.
  • Semicolon: She loved reading; she hated writing essays.

FANBOYS ‚Äì Coordinating Conjunctions

For, And, Nor, But, Or, Yet, So
Use a comma before FANBOYS when it joins two complete sentences.

Common Comma Rules

  • After an introductory word/phrase: After the game, we went home.
  • In a series: We bought apples, bread, and milk.
  • Around nonessential information: My brother, who is in college, loves math.
  • Between city and state / day and year: Boston, Massachusetts; June 3, 2025

üí° Vocabulary, Diction & Style

Using Context Clues

Types of context clues:
  • Definition: The word is explained. The arboretum, a place where many trees are grown, was beautiful.
  • Synonym: Similar word nearby. The task was arduous, or very difficult.
  • Antonym: Opposite word nearby. Unlike his gregarious brother, Sam was quiet.
  • Example: Examples help define the word. Celestial bodies, such as the sun and moon, filled the sky.

Denotation vs. Connotation

Denotation
Dictionary definition of a word.
"Home" = a place where one lives.
Connotation
Feelings/associations a word carries.
"Home" = warmth, comfort, family.

Formal vs. Informal Diction

Informal (avoid in essays): kids, stuff, gonna, a lot, cool, things
Formal (use in academic writing): children, materials, going to, many/much, effective/interesting, items/details
